CRM Continuity

We know firsthand that using a spreadsheet to keep track of contacts and leads is time-consuming, especially when you don't have many leads in the first place. This is why we created a CRM. To help save time and get more done in the same amount of time.

How?

It suggests contacts based on machine learning so that it's easy for you to reach out and start conversations with people who are ready to buy from you. An intuitive interface allows that tracks sales opportunities and manages pipelines.

CRM Secret Sauce

A CRM can help you understand your customers in a more accurate and detailed way than what's available through your website or other customer touch points.

For example, a lead may visit the site three times before they fill out a web form, but if they're never asked for their phone number or address during that process, it might take them months to buy from you. A well-designed CRM will keep track of this information for future reference—and give sales reps access to it when necessary.

1prospect preferences

Learn more about your top prospects and their preferences. Find out how to better interact. For example, if a customer is interested in a certain product but doesn't want to pay the full price, offer a discount code or even a rebate on the item once they purchase at full price.

2Analytics

Use analytics to understand what's happening with your customers. Analytics help you identify your best customers, find patterns in customer interactions, predict customer behavior, and measure the value of each customer.

3Single Source

Keep track of your contacts and their interactions with you. If someone calls or emails you about something, you can look back in the CRM and see everything that has happened with that contact.
